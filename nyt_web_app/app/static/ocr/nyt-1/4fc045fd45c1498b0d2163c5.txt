Victor Hugo and Juliette Drouet.
Tlie Victor Hugo volume which G. P. Putnam’s Sons have in active preparation is not simply a collection of letters, but deals with Victor Hugo and his attachment to Juliette Drouet. It is entitled " The Romance of Victor Hugo and Juliotte Drouet," and is from the pen of Henry Wellington Wack. While in Ger-: many Mr. Wack came across a number | of letters written by Mme. Drouet to i Hugo, and these epistles he has embodied in a sketch of the life of the “ King of Romance ” at Guernsey, where his long exile was spent, and the scene of his most active literary period. Th6 letters arc now to be published for the first time. M. Francois Coppge has written ah introduction to the book. In which he relates many anecdotes of Hugo and his contemporaries. Mr. Wack also gives new information concerning the domestic life of the poet, much of which material be obtained from people in Guernsey who personally knew Huso. The book is fully illustrated.
