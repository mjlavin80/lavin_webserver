The American Book Company Ls bringing- out a ** Commercial Geography," for high schools and colleges, by Henry Gannett, C. L. Garrison, and E. J. Houston. The volume has been divided into three parts—“ Commercial Conditions;*1" giving a statement of the physical, social, and economic conditions that influence commerce in every region; *' Commercial Products,’* which treats of the cultivation of the soil and tho vegetable, animal, and mineral products that enter commerce, and “ Commercial Countries,’* the largest division of tho book, devoted to a description of each country of the world, with special reference to its industries and commerce, both domestic and foreign. Statistical tables have been put at the end of the book, which has also been supplied with many maps and diagrams.
