An article on " Unionizing the School Teachers." by David Swing Ricker, is the leading feature of the April number of The World To-day. It relates, of course, to the Chicago School Teachers’ Federation, its alliance with labor unions, and the results of that alliance. In the same magazine Edgar A. Bancroft discusses railroad ratemaking, Hamilton Wright has a paper on “ The Spineless Cactus,’’ and Wolf von Schierbrand writes about " The Advisers of the Czar." Another feature Is a story of President William H. Harper of the University of Chicago, by Prof. Shailer Mathews, also of Chicago.
