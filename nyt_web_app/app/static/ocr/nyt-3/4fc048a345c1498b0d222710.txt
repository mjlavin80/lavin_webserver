Doubleday, Page & Co. have on their early April list of publications an anniversary edition of Henry'George's " Progress and Poverty." This year is the twenty-fifth anniversary of the issue of the- book, which has appeared in every European country, and in China, Japan, and India. The volume will contain, an introduction by Henry George, Jr., and on- the cover will be a reproduction of the medallion made by his brother Richard, which was the souvenir of the anniversary dinner
held In this city recently. " The Life of Henry George,” by his son, will appear shortly in a uniform edition with *' Progress and Poverty.”
