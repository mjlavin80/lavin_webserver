Bibliography, Citations, and Works Cited in Zotero

  • Review by watching "Using Zotero for academic writing" (and others if needed)
  • Competencies:
    • Add a resource
    • Hand-correct a resource
    • Create a collection
    • Move an item to a collection
  • Bibliography and Export
  • Competencies:
    • Export notes from selected
    • Create bibliography from items
    • .rtf format to word
    • .html to web
    • Generate report from items
  • Word plugin
  • Competencies:
    • Insert citation
    • Edit a citation
    • Refresh a citation
    • Generate works cites from citations
    • Switch citation styles aftet the fact

    • Activity! (Work individually or in pairs to test competencies)

    • Add items for our readings for tonight to new collection of your own
    • Hand-correct citations if necessary
    • In a new Word document, type one quotation or idea you'd cite from each source
    • Use add-on to add parenthetical citations (MLA) for each quotation
    • Generate MLA-format Works Cited
    • Convert all to Chicago style 16th edition (full note, endnote) format

    Activity! (Work as a team to integrate gamification principles)

    • Discussion of crucial gamification principles
    • As a class, brainstorm a lesson plan that unifies Zotero and gamification. Your lesson plan might include homework, a major assignment, or even take place across an entire unit
    • Examine Matt's sample lesson plan, Matt's lesson plan repository, lesson plan template, and other lesson plans
    • Generate for imagined Zotero lesson: "Subject Areas or Affiliated Unit", "Crux or Essential Question of the Lesson", "Learning Objectives", and "Summary"
    • If time: discuss how the efficacy of the lesson would be best assessed
